Our Story

Built for the Episode community.

Buddies Support Hub was founded in August 2022 by Xavier with a single belief; that quality storytelling on Episode deserves recognition, regardless of an author's follower count, experience level, or visibility on the platform.

What started as a small, passionate operation has grown into a structured team of 22 members across four active teams, running free services year-round and hosting the platform's most structured annual competitions in SOTY and AOTY.

We are not a popularity contest. Everything the Hub does, from shoutouts to reviews to monthly contests to SOTY is built around one question: what does exceptional storytelling on Episode actually look like, and how do we make sure it gets seen?

That question hasn't changed since day one. And it won't.

What We Stand For

Three things that never change.

Every decision the Hub makes comes back to these three principles.

01
Quality over popularity
We evaluate stories on craft; plot, directing, writing, interactivity, not follower counts. An author with 500 followers has the same shot at SOTY as one with 10,000. The work speaks for itself here.
02
Free, always
Every service the Hub offers; shoutouts, reviews, contest entries is completely free. We exist to support the community, not profit from it. That commitment has never wavered and never will.
03
Honest and fair
Our feedback is constructive, not flattering. Our judging is rubric-based, not personal. Every author who engages with us deserves the same standard of honesty, care, and respect; and that is what we deliver.

History

Three years and growing.

Key moments in the Hub's journey from a small community idea to a recognized Episode institution.

August 2022
The Hub is Founded
Xavier launches Buddies Support Hub with the goal of giving Episode authors a structured, merit-based route to recognition. The first services; shoutouts and reviews go live.
2022
First SOTY Competition
The inaugural Story of the Year competition launches, spotlighting over 20 stories. Boarding Incomplete wins the first-ever SOTY title, a landmark moment for the Hub and the community.
2023
AOTY Launches
Author of the Year is introduced alongside SOTY, celebrating the author behind the story. Fern becomes the first-ever AOTY winner. The competition is integrated into SOTY in its debut season.
2024
AOTY Becomes Standalone
AOTY launches as a fully independent competition with its own structure, judging criteria, and 40-author field. Polaroids wins SOTY 2024. Ryan Graye wins AOTY 2024.
2025
The Special Opportunity Slot
A new SOTY qualification pathway is introduced; the Special Opportunity Slot, giving authors a direct application route into the competition. Only Pretend wins SOTY 2025. R. Lewis wins AOTY 2025.
2026
Season Ongoing
The 2026 season is underway. Monthly contests are running, qualification for SOTY 2026 is open, and the Hub continues to grow in both team strength and community reach.
Join the Team

Want to be part of this?

We recruit on an as-needed basis throughout the year. When a position opens, it's announced on our Instagram page.

Joining the Buddies Support Hub team is not a casual arrangement, it is a role that carries the Hub's name, reputation, and values. Every member goes through a structured recruitment process that includes age verification and role-specific qualification.

We intentionally keep the team small. Every new member added must be the right person for the role; ready, qualified, and aligned with what the Hub stands for. We recruit when there is a genuine need, not to grow headcount.

If a position opens that matches your skills and interests, you're welcome to apply through the form below.

01
Follow us on Instagram All open positions are announced publicly on @episode_buddies when they become available.
02
Submit your application Use the relevant form for the role you're applying to. Age verification is required for all roles, no exceptions.
03
48-hour response The administration team reviews all applications and responds within 48 hours.
04
Role qualification Review and Contest roles require a qualification process of up to two weeks before onboarding is complete.
